Presenters In Rails

Keeping things organized

Mr Rogers / @rcode5

What is a Presenter?

An object that typically is constructed at the Controller level to manage display or presentation business logic of a model or combination of models.

Why do we need these?

If we try to follow the "Single Responsibility Principle"

every class should have a single responsibility, and that responsibility should be entirely encapsulated by the class. All its services should be narrowly aligned with that responsibility
we'd like to keep display logic encapsulated.

Rails is designed to follow a database table from the database straight through to the view with little or no knowledge of other models. This does not work for all domains.

By building a presenter layer, we have another spot where we can test business logic.
